# U-012: Surface current-day data health warnings

## Objective

Make the real Live and day-detail experiences trustworthy by surfacing clear,
calm warnings when current-day provider data appears stale, incomplete, or suspicious.

This story turned the prototype trust states into real Live-screen behavior driven
by shared health heuristics from the provider-backed current-day payload.

## What Shipped

- Shared current-day health metadata now includes:
  - uptime percentage
  - expected vs covered minutes
  - a list of outage incidents
  - a derived primary incident for top-level warning copy
- The Live screen now:
  - distinguishes healthy, stale, warning, and disconnected cases from real data
  - persists per-incident warning dismissals in the browser
  - keeps dismissed incidents reviewable from the data-quality modal
  - exposes a clickable uptime pill in the trust strip
  - shows all detected outage periods for the selected day
- Current-day coverage logic now:
  - avoids treating the in-progress current minute as missing
  - handles DST-valid local timelines correctly, including repeated fallback hours
  - works around the legacy V1 proxy DST-boundary quirk where shifted clock values can
    be paired with stale calendar-date fields

## Out of Scope

- No full Data Health screen implementation
- No email or push alerting
- No background job orchestration
- No persisted historical outage model beyond current-day Live behavior

## Notes

- This story intentionally stopped at the Live screen and the shared current-day payload.
- Future outage/anomaly persistence, historical uptime reporting, and notification work now
  belongs under `FE-002`.
